# Geographic Mismatch Analysis
# Who Really Funds America's Tax-Writing Committees?

*Analysis generated February 27, 2026 | Data: FEC 2024 cycle (Jan 2023 – Dec 2024)*

---

## Executive Summary

The 71 members of the House Ways and Means Committee and Senate Finance Committee — the legislators who write America's tax code — received a **median of 76%** of their itemized individual campaign contributions from outside their home district or state in the 2024 election cycle.

- **Total itemized contributions analyzed:** $103.0M
- **Mean outside funding:** 74.6%
- **Median outside funding:** 75.8%
- **Mean DC/K-Street funding:** 4.6%
- **Median unitemized gap:** 3.8% (share of total fundraising not geographically analyzable)

---

## Methodology

**Data source:** FEC bulk individual contribution data (Schedule A), 2024 election cycle.

**What's included:**
- Itemized individual contributions (>$200 aggregate per cycle)
- Earmarked contributions via ActBlue/WinRed (attributed to original contributor)

**What's NOT included:**
- Unitemized contributions (<$200) — no geographic data available
- PAC contributions (analyzed separately)
- Joint fundraising committee transfers
- Party committee contributions
- Independent expenditures / dark money

**Geographic matching:** ZIP codes mapped to congressional districts using Census Bureau ZCTA-to-CD crosswalk. ~15% of ZIPs span multiple districts; these are assigned to the majority-population district.

**DC/K-Street:** Contributions from DC-area ZIP codes (200xx–205xx) are classified separately as a proxy for political/lobbying class donors.

1. Only itemized contributions (>$200) are geographically analyzable
